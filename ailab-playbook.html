06 — Content ideas

Steal-able content angles

A few starting points if you are not sure what to write. Adapt these to your voice and your audience.

"I graded my last five email campaigns. Here is what I found."

Run your own emails through the AI Email Grader and share honest results. First-person tool reviews drive real engagement.

Data commentary

"This chart from ActiveCampaign's AI Lab changed how I think about subject lines."

Take one Chart of the Week finding and build your perspective around it. Add your own experience to make it original.

Resource roundup

"Five free AI tools for email marketers worth bookmarking in 2026."

Include AI Lab's tools in a broader roundup — not just an AC post, but a genuinely useful list your audience will save.

Newsletter feature

"This week's free resource worth ten minutes of your time."

A simple, low-friction mention in a newsletter or weekly roundup. Link to a specific tool or chart, not just the homepage.

07 — Guidance

Dos and don'ts

A few principles to keep your AI Lab promotions credible and on-brand.

Do
✓ Lead with your audience's problem, not the product
✓ Test the tools before recommending them
✓ Share your honest take — including limitations
✓ Link to specific tools, charts, or playbooks — not just the homepage
✓ Add your own perspective on top of AI Lab data
✓ Include an affiliate disclosure when applicable
Do not
✗ Promote AI Lab if it is not relevant to the content you are publishing
✗ Make claims you cannot back up ("the best email tool ever")
✗ Reproduce full charts or data visualizations without permission
✗ Imply an official endorsement beyond your affiliate relationship
✗ Alter or recreate the ActiveCampaign logo
✗ Use AI Lab content as filler — it works best when it earns its place
