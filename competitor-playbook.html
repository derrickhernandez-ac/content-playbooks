Creating Quality Competitor Content

Quality content drives results

High-quality, well-researched competitor content does not just rank better — it converts better. Focus on creating genuinely helpful comparisons that serve your audience, and the conversions (and commissions) will follow.

What makes great competitor content?

  • Comprehensive comparison: Covers at least three to five key areas (automation, pricing, deliverability, AI features, integrations, use cases)
  • Fair and balanced: Acknowledges competitor strengths while clearly showing ActiveCampaign's advantages — builds credibility
  • Data-driven: Includes verifiable stats, screenshots, third-party sources, and real examples
  • Audience-focused: Helps readers determine which platform fits their specific needs and use cases
  • Clear CTAs: Includes your affiliate link naturally throughout the content

SEO and AEO best practices

📊 Target high-intent keywords

Focus on comparison keywords with commercial intent:

  • "ActiveCampaign vs [Competitor]"
  • "[Competitor] alternative"
  • "Switch from [Competitor] to ActiveCampaign"
  • "[Competitor] vs ActiveCampaign for [use case or industry]"

🎯 Optimize for AI Overviews (AEO)

AI-generated search results favor content that directly answers questions:

  • Use clear headings: Structure content with H2 and H3 tags that match common questions
  • Answer questions directly: Start paragraphs with clear answers, then elaborate
  • Include comparison tables: AI favors structured data for comparison queries
  • Cite sources: Link to third-party validation (EmailTooltester, G2, customer stories)
  • Feature FAQ sections: Address common questions explicitly

📝 Content structure for rankings

Recommended article structure (1,500–2,500 words)
  1. Introduction (150–200 words): State the comparison clearly, include primary keyword
  2. Quick verdict (100–150 words): "Choose X if... Choose Y if..." format
  3. Comparison table: Side-by-side features, pricing, and key metrics
  4. Feature deep-dives (800–1,200 words): Four to six detailed sections covering automation, deliverability, pricing, AI features, and so on
  5. Customer stories (200–300 words): Real results from businesses that switched
  6. FAQ section (300–400 words): Five to seven common questions
  7. Final recommendation (100–150 words): Clear conclusion with CTA

🎥 Video content for YouTube SEO

  • Title optimization: Include both product names and year (e.g., "ActiveCampaign vs. Mailchimp 2026: complete comparison")
  • Timestamps: Add chapter markers in description for each comparison area
  • Show, do not just tell: Screen recordings of both platforms side-by-side
  • Hook in first 30 seconds: State who each platform is best for
  • Pin affiliate link: Include in pinned comment and description

Content quality checklist

Before publishing, ensure your content:
  • ✓ Includes at least three direct competitor comparisons with specific examples
  • ✓ Features screenshots or visuals showing both platforms
  • ✓ Cites at least two third-party sources (G2, EmailTooltester, customer reviews)
  • ✓ Links to ActiveCampaign customer success stories for proof
  • ✓ Acknowledges what the competitor does well (builds trust)
  • ✓ Provides clear "choose X if..." guidance for readers
  • ✓ Includes your affiliate link three to five times naturally throughout
  • ✓ Uses switch landing page deep-links where appropriate

What to avoid

  • Thin content: Generic listicles without depth or original insights
  • One-sided criticism: Only highlighting competitor weaknesses damages credibility
  • Outdated information: Always verify pricing, features, and stats are current
  • Pure AI generation: Add human expertise, testing, and original analysis
  • No visuals: Comparison content needs screenshots or diagrams
