The mindset shift

You are not here to promote AI Lab. You are here to use it — and let better content do the promoting for you. The affiliates who will get the most out of AI Lab are the ones who treat it as a research tool first and a referral opportunity second.

Think of AI Lab activation as a three-stage journey. Each stage builds on the last — and each one pays off for you before it pays off for us.

Stage 1 — Use it yourself first

Before you recommend anything to your audience, get your own hands on it. Run your last five emails through the AI Email Grader. Pull your industry's benchmarks. Test a subject line. The content ideas will come naturally once you have real results to talk about.

What to do in Stage 1
  • Run your own email campaigns through the AI Email Grader and note what surprised you
  • Check the Chart of the Week — does it confirm or challenge something you already believed?
  • Pull your industry's data from the Industry Benchmarker
  • Read one piece of original research relevant to your niche
Why this matters

First-person experience is the only thing that makes tool content trustworthy. "I tested this on my own campaigns" converts at a completely different rate than "here is a tool you should check out."

Stage 2 — Let it improve your existing content

You are already writing about email marketing, automation, and marketing strategy. AI Lab gives you data, charts, and research findings that make those posts more original and more credible — without changing your topic or your voice.

What to do in Stage 2
  • Reference a Chart of the Week finding in a newsletter you were already planning to send
  • Open a blog post with a stat from AI Lab's research instead of a generic industry claim
  • Add a before-and-after subject line comparison to a post about email copywriting
  • Use your industry benchmark results as original data in a niche-specific post
  • Cite AI Lab as the source — the link back builds credibility for you and visibility for AI Lab
The compounding effect

Every piece of content you publish with AI Lab data in it is a potential entry point for a reader to discover ActiveCampaign. Your affiliate link does the rest — you do not need to make a separate ask.

Stage 3 — Point your audience toward what's useful

Once you have used AI Lab yourself and referenced it in your content, sending your audience directly to a specific tool or playbook is a natural next step. At this point it is a recommendation, not a promotion — and your audience will feel the difference.

What to do in Stage 3
  • Link to the specific AI tool you wrote about — not just the AI Lab homepage
  • Recommend a playbook when it genuinely fits a topic you are covering — it is free, useful, and gated by email
  • Share a Chart of the Week reaction on LinkedIn or X with your own take and tag ActiveCampaign
  • If you are willing, share a short quote on a chart or finding with the affiliate team — we may feature it on AI Lab, which drives visibility back to you
Keep it contextual

A specific recommendation tied to content your audience just read will always outperform a generic "check out this resource" mention. If it does not fit naturally, wait until it does.

Experience first, content second

Three tools are live now, all free, all built on data from 8.3 million real campaigns. Use them on your own work before you mention them to your audience. The results — surprising, validating, or somewhere in between — are the content.

🔍 AI Email Grader

The question it answers for you: How do my emails actually hold up against what's working across 8.3 million campaigns?

Run any email through the grader and get an instant A–F audit across six dimensions: subject line, copy, design, mobile, deliverability, and accessibility. The score is concrete, shareable, and almost always surprising in at least one area.

What tends to happen when you use it

Most people expect to score well on the things they obsess over and poorly on the things they ignore. The grader usually flips that — which is exactly the kind of result worth writing about. "I thought my subject lines were the problem. Turns out it was mobile formatting" is a better post than any tool review.

Content Ideas

An honest note on commissions

AI Lab does not have a checkout page, a free trial, or a pricing tier. That means content purely about AI Lab will not directly earn you commissions. We are telling you this upfront because we think a strategy you understand is more valuable than one you have to reverse-engineer yourself.

The content ideas below are split into two types — and understanding the difference is the key to making AI Lab work for you as an affiliate.

Type 1 — Authority builders

Pure AI Lab content. No commission, but builds your credibility and audience trust. These posts make your Type 2 content convert better.

Type 2 — Commission earners

Bridge content that connects AI Lab data to ActiveCampaign. This is where your affiliate link lives and where commissions are earned.

Type 1 — Authority builders

These posts earn you nothing directly. What they earn you is something more valuable long-term — a reputation as someone who publishes original, data-backed content your audience can trust. That trust is what makes your Type 2 content convert.

Tool review

"I graded my last five email campaigns with AI. Here's what I found."

Run your own emails through the AI Email Grader and share honest results — score, breakdown, and what surprised you. First-person tool reviews with real data outperform generic recommendations every time.

Data commentary

"This chart changed how I think about email send times."

Take one Chart of the Week finding, add your own interpretation, and publish it. You are not summarizing AI Lab — you are adding a perspective that only you can provide.

Benchmark breakdown

"How does my industry's email performance actually compare? I used this free tool to find out."

Pull your industry's numbers from the Industry Benchmarker and share what they mean for your specific niche. Original data for a specific audience is some of the most shareable content you can produce.

Subject line experiment

"I ran 10 of my subject lines through an AI optimizer. Here's what it flagged."

Before-and-after subject line comparisons are highly shareable and genuinely useful. The patterns the tool flags across multiple lines are more interesting than any single suggestion.

Type 2 — Commission earners

These posts use AI Lab data as the foundation and ActiveCampaign as the conclusion. The bridge between them has to be honest — the data should genuinely support the recommendation, not just provide cover for an affiliate pitch.

The data-to-platform bridge

"AI Lab's research shows the average open rate in my industry is X. Here's the platform I use to beat it."

Lead with a specific AI Lab finding, explain what it means for your audience, and then introduce ActiveCampaign as the tool that helps you hit or exceed that benchmark. Your affiliate link belongs at the end of that argument, not at the beginning.

The deliverability angle

"I tested my emails with AI Lab's grader. The biggest problem wasn't my copy — it was my platform."

If the Email Grader surfaces deliverability or design issues that your current tool can't solve, ActiveCampaign is a natural next step. This only works if it is true for you — do not manufacture a problem that does not exist.

The benchmark-to-trial bridge

"Industry benchmarks say X. ActiveCampaign users average Y. Here's how to close the gap."

Use Industry Benchmarker data to establish what good looks like, then show how ActiveCampaign's features help get there. Link to a free trial with your affiliate link as the CTA.

The research roundup

"5 things AI Lab's data taught me about email marketing in 2026 — and the tool I use because of them."

Aggregate several AI Lab findings into a single post and let the cumulative weight of the data lead naturally to an ActiveCampaign recommendation. Multiple data points make the conclusion feel earned rather than forced.

The two-post play

The most effective approach is to publish a Type 1 post first — a genuine, no-pitch AI Lab piece that builds credibility with your audience. Then publish a Type 2 post that references the same data and bridges to ActiveCampaign. Your audience already trusts you on the topic. The affiliate link converts because the authority was established first.

Dos and Don'ts

A few principles to keep your AI Lab content credible, effective, and worth your audience's time.

✅ Do

  • Lead with your audience's problem, not the product. If your content starts with "AI Lab is a great resource," it reads like an ad. If it starts with "here is what the data says about email open rates," it reads like journalism.
  • Test the tools before recommending them. Your first-hand experience is the only thing that makes tool content genuinely trustworthy. Run your own campaigns through the tools first.
  • Share your honest take — including limitations. If a tool surprised you in a bad way, say so. That candor is what makes your recommendations worth reading.
  • Link to specific tools or charts, not just the AI Lab homepage. A contextual link to a specific resource will always outperform a generic "check this out" mention.
  • Add your own perspective on top of AI Lab data. Rephrasing what the page already says adds no value. Your interpretation — especially for your specific niche — is what your audience is coming for.
  • Let Type 1 content earn the right for Type 2. Publish a genuine AI Lab piece first. Build the credibility. Then bridge to ActiveCampaign when it is earned — that is when your affiliate link converts.
  • Include an affiliate disclosure when applicable. Required where your jurisdiction mandates it, and builds trust even where it does not.

❌ Do not

  • Force an ActiveCampaign affiliate link into AI Lab content where it does not belong. AI Lab is a credibility builder, not a conversion page. Shoehorning an affiliate pitch into a data post undermines both. Let the two-post play work as designed.
  • Write about AI Lab content you have not actually used. If you have not run your own campaigns through the tools, your audience will feel it. Generic tool coverage is easy to spot and easy to ignore.
  • Make claims you cannot back up. Stick to what the data says and what you have personally tested. Do not extrapolate beyond what AI Lab's research actually shows.
  • Reproduce full charts or data visualizations without permission. Reference them, link to them, add your take — but do not screengrab and republish without checking with us first.
  • Imply an official endorsement beyond your affiliate relationship. You are a partner sharing a resource — not a spokesperson for ActiveCampaign or AI Lab.
  • Use AI Lab content as filler. If it does not genuinely fit what you are publishing, leave it out. One strong, relevant reference beats three forced mentions every time.
  • Alter or recreate the ActiveCampaign logo. Use brand assets as provided.
